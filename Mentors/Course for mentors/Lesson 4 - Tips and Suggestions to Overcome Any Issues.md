# Tips and Suggestions to Overcome Any Issues

Even with the best planning, mentoring students in an open-source program can present challenges. Here are some practical tips to help you navigate common issues and keep your students engaged and successful.

## **1\. Student Not Engaging**

* **Identify early signs:** Missing weekly meetings, delayed updates, or minimal interaction on Slack.  
* **Reach out proactively:** Send a friendly message to check in and understand any obstacles. Sometimes students may feel stuck or intimidated.  
* **Break tasks into smaller steps:** Simplifying work into achievable pieces can help them regain momentum.  
* **2 weeks with no response from the student after 4 or more communication attempts:** Inform the WordPress Credits admin team for internal follow up ([internships@wordpressfoundation.org](mailto:internships@wordpressfoundation.org)). 

### 2\. Student Overwhelmed or Behind Schedule

* **Review their workload:** Some students may underestimate the time needed for tasks. Help them reassess priorities and break tasks into manageable chunks.  
* **Set mini-deadlines:** Weekly or even daily targets can prevent last-minute rushes.  
* **Offer guidance on time management:** Share your own strategies for estimating effort and organizing work.  
* **Celebrate small wins:** Recognizing progress, no matter how minor, boosts confidence and motivation.

### 3\. Student Changing Project Scope

* **Listen first:** Understand why they want to pivot. It could be a genuine learning opportunity or misalignment with their goals.  
* **Connect with the right resources:** If the new project is outside your expertise, introduce them to a contributor or team familiar with that area.  
* **Ensure feasibility:** Help them scope the new project realistically so it can be completed within the program timeline.  
* **Don’t discard the first contributions:** Encourage the student to keep in the project website any content they have created under their first project scope, and share the reasoning for changing it in the following posts. 

### 4\. Technical or Workflow Challenges

* **Encourage peer support:** Point them to Slack channels, forums, or documentation where they can get help from the community.  
* **Provide step-by-step guidance when needed:** Sometimes a small demonstration or example is enough to unblock them.  
* **Document solutions:** Encourage them to note down and publish the solutions for future reference, this also helps the community.

### 5\. Maintaining Motivation and Confidence

* **Set clear expectations:** Students perform best when they understand what “done” looks like for each task.  
* **Provide encouragement regularly:** Positive feedback reinforces effort and learning.  
* **Model open-source collaboration:** Show patience, curiosity, and transparency. Students often mirror the mentor’s behavior.

Please, take a look at the [Mentors guide](https://make.wordpress.org/handbook/wordpress-credits-contribution-internship-program/a-guide-for-wp-credits-mentors/) for more tips and suggestions.
