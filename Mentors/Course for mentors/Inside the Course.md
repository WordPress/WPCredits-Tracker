# Inside the Course

Before you begin mentoring, it’s important to understand what your students will experience inside the **WordPress Credits course**.

Here you’ll find a short **“walk-through” video** that shows the course from a student’s perspective — how it looks, how it flows, and what kind of activities they’ll complete week by week.

[https://wordpress.tv/2025/10/02/how-to-navigate-the-wordpress-credits-course/](https://wordpress.tv/2025/10/02/how-to-navigate-the-wordpress-credits-course/)

For more information about the student journey please read the [student guide](https://make.wordpress.org/handbook/wordpress-credits-contribution-internship-program/student-guide/). 

## **The course structure**

The student course is divided into **three main sections**:

### 1\. Onboarding

In this first stage, students learn the fundamentals of WordPress as an open-source project.

They will go through several short courses hosted on [learn.wordpress.org](https://learn.wordpress.org/), where they’ll gain the necessary background before starting their contribution project.

### 2\. Project

Once onboarding is complete, students move on to the **Project phase** — the heart of the program.

Here, they:

* Identify the area of WordPress they want to contribute to  
* Plan and develop their own **contribution project**, aligned with their studies and interests  
* Create a personal WordPress site where they share updates and reflections on their contribution work  
* Join the WordPress.org Slack, attend team meetings, and, when possible, participate in local or online WordPress events

As a mentor, you’ll support them through this phase by reviewing progress, discussing goals, and helping them connect with the right teams or contributors.

### 3\. Wrap-up

In the final stage, students reflect on what they’ve learned and showcase their work.  
They will:

* Record a short **presentation video** summarizing their experience and outcomes  
* Upload it to **WordPress.tv**  
* Embed it on their personal website along with a final reflection post

This marks the completion of their journey as WordPress Credits participants — and the beginning of their path as open-source contributors.

## **Course Flow and Practical Details**

* Each lesson ends with a **form (blue button)** that students must complete before unlocking the next lesson.  
* You will have access to an **Airtable** \- table where you will be able to **view your students replies** to those forms.   
* Students **cannot skip ahead**; they must finish each lesson before moving forward.  
* The expected rhythm is **one lesson per week**, allowing students to balance learning, contribution, and reflection.  
* If they have any **questions or issues**, you, as their **mentor, are their first point of contact**. Encourage them to reach out proactively whenever they feel stuck or uncertain.
